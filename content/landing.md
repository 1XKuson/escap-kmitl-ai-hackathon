### 💡 The Challenge: The Governance Gap

**The Speed of Trade vs. The Speed of Law**
Digital trade moves at light speed while regulation battles bureaucracy. While digital data flows at velocities exceeding 50 TB/s, national policy adaptation cycles remain trapped in a manual, 20th-century update process that often spans years or decades. This disparity creates "Invisible Trade Barriers".

**The "Shredded Map" Reality**
Currently, regulatory data is locked in unstructured PDF or HTML formats. When standard AI tools extract this information, they treat it as plain text strings, destroying the logical hierarchy, nested clauses, and table logic. This loss of structure leads to dangerous AI hallucinations and broken legal logic.

**The Objective**
We must move from a "shredded map" approach to a "whole map" solution. Your goal is to build a working, modular AI tool (Automated Regulatory Engine) that helps identify, classify, and summarize digital trade regulatory provisions from official documents.

***

### 🎯 Focus Areas: High-Impact Policy Pillars

The competition focuses on two highly complex and rapidly changing pillars of the Regional Digital Trade Integration Index (RDTII):

*   **Pillar 6: Cross-Border Data Policies** (e.g., Ban & local processing, Data localization mandates, Conditional flow regimes)
*   **Pillar 7: Domestic Data Protection & Privacy** (e.g., Legal framework gaps, Mandatory data retention periods, Compliance costs)

**The Solution You Need to Build**
Participants are expected to build an AI-assisted RegTech prototype utilizing **HtmlRAG and Structural Intelligence** to preserve legal hierarchies. The pipeline should include:
*   **Module A — Identification & Extraction:** Detect relevant passages and output exact spans with accurate source-grounded citations.
*   **Module B — Policy Classification:** Assign provisions to predefined policy categories with an AI rationale.
*   **Module C — Policy Summarization:** Generate concise, plain-language summaries highlighting regulatory intent for non-technical officials.
